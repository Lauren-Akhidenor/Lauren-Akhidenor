## Overview
----
A data analyst and agricultural economist focused on **food systems, price transmission, and structural drivers of food security**.

I apply **econometrics, time-series modelling, and data engineering** to analyse how agricultural and economic systems shape **food affordability, accessibility, and stability** at both household and national levels.

My work focuses on how structural forces in food systems translate into measurable outcomes in food security.

Core focus areas:

- Economic modelling of price transmission  
- Data-driven development and analytics  
- Sustainability and system efficiency in agriculture

## 🏛️ Anchor Projects (Food Accessibility & Affordability Focus)
------
I analyse how macroeconomic forces, particularly inflation, fuel prices, and input costs, transmit through agricultural systems, shaping:

- Consumer food price dynamics  
- Farmer income stability  
- Household food security outcomes    

📌 To explore each project, click below to expand.

<details>
<summary>The Fuel Lag Effect: A Time-Series Analysis of UK Food Inflation Drivers</summary>

- **Impact:** Fuel-driven food inflation places sustained pressure on households, with low price elasticity (~ -0.19), limiting consumer adjustment
- **Insight:** Diesel price shocks transmit into UK food inflation with a 6 - 9 month lag, most strongly affecting bread, fish, and vegetables
- **Tools:** Excel | Python | Power Bi
- **Method:** Time-series econometric modelling (VECM + cointegration + lag analysis)
- **Output:** Interactive dashboard + transmission analysis of fuel-to-food price dynamics

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Fuel_and_Food_Price_Analysis-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/Fuel-and-Food-Price-Analysis/blob/main/README.md)

</details>

<details>
<summary>The Price Transmission Gap: Inflation vs Agricultural Producer Returns (2019 - 2025)</summary>

- **Impact:** Consumer inflation does not consistently translate into higher producer earnings, exposing structural income gaps for farmers in developing economies
- **Insight:** Significant price transmission gaps exist across countries, with weak alignment in Nigeria and Egypt, and stronger synchronisation in the UK, USA, Netherlands, and Ireland
- **Tools:** Power Bi
- **Method:** Comparative economic analysis using FAOSTAT data with cross-country benchmarking of inflation vs producer price movements (Power BI modelling)
- **Output:** Interactive dashboard comparing inflation, producer prices, and crop-level production (wheat vs potatoes) across 8 countries

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Comparison_Analysis_FAO_-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/comparsion-analysis-fao-/)

</details>

## 🏛️ Ancillary Projects (Food Availability, Utilisation & Stability Focus)
------

I also examine broader agricultural system dynamics that underpin food security beyond pricing, including:

- Food availability and production efficiency  
- System stability and resilience  
- Sustainability, input optimisation, and risk management

  
📌 To explore each project, click below to expand.


<details>
<summary>The Food System Inequality Engine: Global Agricultural Productivity & Risk (2015 - 2029)</summary>

- **Impact:** Global food insecurity is driven more by system inefficiency than production capacity, with structural yield gaps (up to 10×) creating persistent vulnerability in developing economies
- **Insight:** Food insecurity is primarily shaped by yield inequality, post-harvest losses, and trade concentration, rather than total global output
- **Tools:** Excel | SQL| SPSS | Python | Power Bi
- **Method:** Integrated data engineering pipeline (Excel → SQL → SPSS → Python → Power BI) with regression analysis and ML-based forecasting (Random Forest)
- **Output:** End-to-end analytical system + interactive dashboard + 2025–2029 food system forecast model

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-_Global_Agricultural_Food_Systems_Analysis_-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/food-productivity-stability)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Smart Farm Inventory System: SQL-Driven Restock & Climate Signal Analytics</summary>

- **Impact:** Built an automated agricultural inventory system that improves procurement decision-making through real-time stock alerts and structured data integrity controls
- **Insight:** Climate expectation signals introduce higher price uncertainty (↑ coefficient of variation), while average pricing remains stable across conditions
- **Tools:** SQL | Power BI
- **Method:** End-to-end SQL Server system design (schema design, triggers, stored procedures, constraints) integrated with Power BI for operational analytics
- **Output:** Automated restock alert system + interactive Power BI dashboard for inventory and pricing monitoring

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Agro_Inventory_&_Climate_Analysis-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/agro-inventory-climate-analysis/blob/main/README.md)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Optimising Agricultural Production Systems Using Linear Programming (Feed, Profit & Capacity Allocation)</summary>

- **Impact:** Demonstrates how constrained optimisation reduces feed costs and maximises agro-processing profit under limited resources
- **Insight:** Agricultural systems operate at binding constraints, profit and efficiency are maximised only when capacity is fully utilised, not when demand changes
- **Tools:** Excel
- **Method:** Linear Programming optimisation using Excel Solver across feed formulation, production scheduling, and machine allocation scenarios
- **Output:** Decision-ready optimisation models for cost minimisation, profit maximisation, and capacity allocation in agricultural systems

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Linear_Programming-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/LINEAR-PROGRAMMING)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Economic Evaluation of Mycorrhizal Inoculation in Cabbage Production (Comparative Experimental Study)</summary>

- **Impact:** Mycorrhizal inoculation improves cabbage production profitability, increasing ROI from 74.8% to 127% under treatment conditions
- **Insight:** Productivity improvements are evident, but economic returns depend on pricing assumptions and market validation rather than yield alone
- **Tools:** Excel
- **Method:** Comparative experimental study (treatment vs control) combined with Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA) and ROI modelling
- **Output:** Agronomic-economic evaluation linking biological soil input performance to farm profitability outcomes

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Cabbage_Production-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/Cost-Benefit-Analysis-Cabbage/blob/main/README.md)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Organic vs Integrated Farming: Cost–Benefit Analysis of Amaranth & Corchorus Production</summary>

- **Impact:** Organic farming outperforms integrated systems economically, generating a positive ROI (19.9%) versus losses (-18.4%) under the same production conditions
- **Insight:** Higher chemical input intensity does not improve profitability, cost escalation outweighs yield gains in integrated systems
- **Tools:** Excel
- **Method:** Comparative Cost–Benefit Analysis (CBA) using yield, cost, revenue, ROI, and benefit–cost ratio modelling across two production systems
- **Output:** Economic evaluation framework comparing organic and integrated smallholder farming systems

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Farm_Profitability-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/CBA2/blob/main/README.md)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Maize Variety Adoption & Agronomic Practices in Embu, Kenya: Visual Analytics Study</summary>

- **Impact:** Reveals a “technology adoption paradox” where high uptake of improved maize varieties (96.6% adoption) does not translate into fully optimised farming practices
- **Insight:** Adoption is driven by access, but productivity is constrained by input costs, pests/diseases, and limited complementary agronomic practices
- - **Tools:** Power Bi
- **Method:** Power BI-based survey analysis using a star schema data model to evaluate adoption patterns, training effects, and production constraints (n=149 households)
- **Output:** Interactive agricultural adoption dashboard identifying behavioural and structural constraints in smallholder maize systems

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Maize_Adoption_Dashboard-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/MAIZE-GENETICS-/blob/main/README.md)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Maize Productivity in Bungoma County, Kenya: Socioeconomic & Agronomic Drivers</summary>

- **Impact:** Transformed household survey data into insights showing that maize productivity is primarily driven by land access and structural farm size rather than experience or farming tenure
- **Insight:** Land size is the strongest determinant of output (r ≈ 0.97), while farming experience and plot-level variables show weak predictive power, indicating scale-driven rather than efficiency-driven production systems
- **Tools:** Excel | SPSS
- **Method:** Combined Excel and SPSS analysis (descriptive statistics, correlation, regression, and t-tests) using a 59-household cross-sectional survey dataset
- **Output:** Socioeconomic and agronomic insight model identifying structural constraints in smallholder maize production systems

[![Project Link](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Bungoma_Kenya_Analysis-F5F5DC?logo=github&logoColor=white&labelColor=800020)](https://github.com/Lauren-Akhidenor/Analysis-for-Bungoma-Kenya)


</details>
